# Interview Snippet 01

> **Status:** This is a fictional placeholder so the repo structure works end-to-end. Before the workshop, this will be replaced with an anonymized excerpt derived from a colleague's transcript, with their consent.

## Context (fictional)

A 28-year-old design researcher (R) interviewing a market vendor (V) about informal infrastructure repair practices in a city neighborhood. Interview conducted in person, in the vendor's stall, mid-afternoon. Audio recorded, transcribed verbatim, names changed.

## Excerpt

**R:** Can you tell me how you decided to fix the awning yourself instead of waiting for the landlord?

**V:** It is not really a decision. You see the rain coming, you do something. I called him twice. He came once, looked, said he would send someone. That was last year.

**R:** And the materials — where did the wood come from?

**V:** From over there [gestures to alley]. The construction across the road, they have a pile. Everyone takes from there. The foreman, he knows. It is understood.

**R:** Understood by whom?

**V:** By people who live here. You would not know unless you asked, which is what you are doing now, no? [laughs]

**R:** Has the landlord said anything since?

**V:** He passed by in March. He looked at the awning and said "you did this well." That was all. He did not offer to pay for the materials. He did not say it was illegal. He said "you did this well." So.

**R:** So... what?

**V:** So nothing. We continue.

## Notes for the model

This excerpt is intentionally short. Useful questions to ask the model alongside it:

- "Where is the researcher framing things in ways the vendor pushes back against?"
- "What is the vendor describing that the researcher doesn't yet have language for?"
- "What's *not* in this excerpt that you'd want to know?"

The point is not to extract the "meaning" of the snippet — it's to notice how a careful interlocutor (model or human) can surface the parts of a transcript where the framing of a question and the framing of the answer don't match.

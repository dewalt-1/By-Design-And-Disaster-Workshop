# Reading List

The references cited in the workshop call, grouped by theme, plus institutional guidelines on the use of AI in academic research.

## Ethics & environment of AI

- Grosser, B. and Pold, S.B. (2025) "Reading the Praise/Prompt Machine: An Interface Criticism Approach to ChatGPT," *Proceedings of the sixth decennial Aarhus conference: Computing X Crisis*. Aarhus N, Denmark: ACM, pp. 259–271. https://doi.org/10.1145/3744169.3744194
- Neuburg, K., Quadflieg, S. and Nestler, S. (2020) "Will artificial intelligence make designers obsolete?" *DAI Conference 2020*, Berlin (remote), pp. 1–6.

## Design research as craft

- Mills, C.W. (1959) "On Intellectual Craftsmanship," in *The Sociological Imagination*. Oxford University Press, pp. 195–226.
- Polanyi, M. (1966) *The Tacit Dimension*. Chicago: The University of Chicago Press.
- Schindler, L. and Schäfer, H. (2021) "Practices of Writing in Ethnographic Work," *Journal of Contemporary Ethnography*, 50(1), pp. 11–32. https://doi.org/10.1177/0891241620923396
- Schön, D.A. (1983) *The Reflective Practitioner: How Professionals Think in Action*. New York: Basic Books.
- Sennett, R. (2008) *The Craftsman*. New Haven, CT: Yale University Press.

## Global South & multilingual LLMs

- Berger, T. (2021) "The 'Global South' as a relational category — global hierarchies in the production of law and legal pluralism," *Third World Quarterly*, 42(9), pp. 2001–2017. https://doi.org/10.1080/01436597.2020.1827948
- Brown, N. and Marivate, V. (2025) "Pula: Training Large Language Models for Setswana." *arXiv*. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2408.02239
- Noor, E. and Kanitroj, B. (2025) "Speaking in Code: Contextualizing Large Language Models in Southeast Asia." Carnegie Endowment for International Peace. https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2025/01/speaking-in-code-contextualizing-large-language-models-in-southeast-asia
- Tonja, A.L. et al. (2024) "InkubaLM: A small language model for low-resource African languages." *arXiv*. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2408.17024
- Zhao, Y. et al. (2024) "How do Large Language Models Handle Multilingualism?" *arXiv*. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2402.18815
